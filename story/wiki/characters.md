# Characters — Life of Bon

---

## Bon (Pre-Reincarnation)

**Role:** Protagonist — Japan identity
**Age at death:** Mid-20s
**State:** Functional depression — maintains outward normalcy while feeling empty

### Key Traits
- Listens to music constantly (emotional armor / isolation tool)
- Works out regularly but feels no satisfaction
- Dead-end jobs, failed interviews, avoids family calls out of shame
- Only genuine connection: neighbor child **Aki** (age 6–7), helps her with homework
- **Death:** Dives in front of a truck to save Aki — his first unambiguously selfless act, done without hesitation or self-doubt

### Internal Voice (template for all of Bon's self-talk)
> "I hate myself! All I do is talk a big game... I can't do anything." — Natsuki Subaru (Re:Zero)
> "Come on, move! You're my body! Why won't you move? ...I hate you! And I always have!" — Armin (AoT)

## Bon (Post-Reincarnation)

**Role:** Protagonist
**Core struggle:** Self-hatred from previous life vs. genuine desire for connection in the new world
**Arc archetype:** Rock Lee — earns everything through work, masters nothing effortlessly

### Key Traits
- Retains all memories from Japan; carries guilt of his past life into every relationship
- Believes he "stole" another child's life by being reborn
- Magic tied to emotion rather than theory — his reservoir is built from a lifetime of surviving pain
- Combat style: practical and dirty — cauterizes wounds with fire, digs trenches, exploits terrain; not a clean duelist
- Magic signature (not yet locked): visually impure — unstable edges, color shifts with emotional state, "works but looks wrong by academic standards"

### Growth Arc (4 axes)
- Self-hatred → Self-acceptance
- Isolation → Genuine connection
- Perfectionism → Growth mindset
- Individual → Community

### Canon Fate
Open. Four options: killed by a hero; retires to old age; dies saving the love interest (Aki bookend); **favorite: dies with a genuine smile, becomes a legend — acceptance achieved at the very end.**

## Elena (Mother)

**Role:** Bon's new-world mother; primary symbol of undeserved love
**Relationship to Bon:** Unconditional warmth — the love he never felt he deserved in Japan

### Key Traits
- Emotionally intelligent, loving
- Runs the herb shop with Leon; Bon learns that usefulness feels good here
- Falls ill from rare imported herbs (ages 7–8) — Bon's first major crisis
- While Leon is away in Brynth, Elena continues Bon's training despite her illness — shows up on bad days
- That image (sick mother dragging herself to train with him) stays with Bon forever

### Canon Fate
Displaced and **enslaved** during the Arc 2 dungeon disaster. Bon does not immediately search for her — that failure mirrors Japan. Finding and freeing her is the core motivation of Arc 3.
- **Still open:** Who enslaved her? Options: human warlord on Dorwin (political captive); criminal underground (sold in the chaos); foreign kingdom (trapped as refugee). Not yet decided.
- Elena never learns Bon ran — she believes the disaster simply separated them. That gap is permanent weight.
- **Open question:** Does Bon ever confess his role in Leon's death to her? If yes, he's finally stopped running. If no, the old pattern isn't dead — just quieter.

## Leon (Father)

**Role:** Bon's new-world father; the father Bon never felt he deserved
**Backstory:** Former skilled adventurer — charismatic, a ladies' man in his youth — who retired after a mission forced a call that cost a party member their life. He was right. He never forgave himself. Both things are true.

### Key Traits
- Hardworking, physically capable, deeply caring
- Teaches Bon basic combat and meditation from age 4
- Teaches with a practicality that has no room for ego — a tell of his past
- Goes still and quiet near danger rather than tense
- Takes dangerous escort missions to Brynth to fund Elena's cure

### Canon Fate
**Dead.** Killed during the Arc 2 dungeon collapse. Bon's fire magic misfires and puts people in danger; Leon runs toward it — his instinct to protect, the same instinct that defined his adventuring life, is what kills him. **He made the call again. This time the mission was his son.**

What this does to Bon: every rank he climbs is haunted by the question — *what's the point of getting stronger if it was my weakness that killed him in the first place?*

## Vaela ("The Unnamed" / "She Who Remains")

**Role:** Main antagonist
**Inspired by:** Ranni, Marika, Frenzied Flame (Elden Ring) — tonal/structural reference only
**Enneagram:** Type 5 — The Investigator

### Naming
| Layer | Name | Who uses it |
|---|---|---|
| What the world calls her | **The Unnamed** | Everyone — not irony, it's ignorance |
| Ancient texts | **She Who Remains** | Pre-dates living memory; what she is, not what she's called |
| Her true name | **Vaela** | Known by essentially nobody alive. Hearing it spoken is a significant plot beat. |

### What She Is
A dying incorporeal being — consciousness without physical anchor, slowly dissolving over centuries. Cannot touch, taste, feel, or be seen. Was something before, but that identity is buried under layers of absorbed memories.

### Origin (Canon)
Before the current world existed, one unified civilization fought a conflict that was literally tearing reality apart. Vaela was not one of the fighters — she loved people who were. When total destruction became certain, she sacrificed herself to contain the damage.

**The betrayal:** The people she saved either knew it would cost her everything and said nothing, or engineered the situation so her sacrifice was the solution they needed. They used her love as the mechanism. She gave everything willingly — which made it the perfect weapon.

The world rebuilt on top of her sacrifice. She was left incorporeal, dissolving, forgotten.

### What She Remembers
Almost nothing clearly. Centuries of absorbing others' memories have buried her own. She gets fragments — a face she can't place, warmth she can't source. Sometimes she can't tell if a memory of grief is hers or someone else's.

She does not know if the betrayal was deliberate. She operates on conviction but cannot fully remember why she started.

### Her Plan
Restructure reality so consciousness can persist without physical form — make physical bodies optional, eliminate physical death.

**Why she's wrong:**
- Identity dissolution: people lose sense of self without physical anchoring
- Connection breakdown: relationships require physical presence to be meaningful
- Experiential death: touch, taste, intimacy all become impossible

### Vaela and Bon (Canon)
When Vaela reads Bon near an anchor, she encounters memories of Japan — a world entirely outside her network. First time she's realized other worlds exist and that consciousness can transfer between them and survive physically. Her model of reality was incomplete.

She cannot fully read his memories (interference). Must keep returning to him — meaning she has reason to keep him alive. His unabsorbed memories are the first thing in centuries that remind her what it felt like to have a memory that was only hers.
