# World Rules — Life of Bon

---

## 1. The World

**Name:** Aetheria (also referred to as Eldarion in some sections)
**Open decision:** Choose one as the world name; the other could be a region, a historical name, or cut. Decide before line-editing the manuscript.

**Origin:** The current world is the rebuilt remnant of a more advanced unified civilization. The ancient conflict between extraordinarily powerful individuals literally tore reality apart — the six continents splitting, the races diverging, the dungeon ruins forming. All of it is the scar tissue of that original war. Vaela's sacrifice contained the damage; the world rebuilt on top of it.

---

## 2. Magic System

### Elemental Schools (4)
- Fire
- Water
- Earth
- Wind

### Power Levels (5)
Beginner → Intermediate → Advanced → Expert → Master

- **Hard-won progression** — Bon earns every level through work; nothing comes effortlessly
- **Silent casting** — available to advanced practitioners; feels hard-won, not given
- **Bon's magic signature (not yet locked):** Visually impure — unstable edges, color that shifts with emotional state, slightly wrong by academic standards. Works. Is powerful. Looks wrong.

### Battle Aura — Touki
Physical enhancement for combat. Separate from elemental magic; used by skilled fighters to boost speed, strength, and durability.

### Bon's Source
His magic is tied to emotion rather than academic theory. The reservoir comes from a lifetime of surviving pain — every time he pushed through and kept going when he was broken. Magic for Bon is emotional endurance made physical.

## 3. Dungeons

- **Nature:** Naturally occurring magical phenomena — not built, not designed
- **Properties:** Bigger on the inside than outside (spatial folding magic)
- **Danger:** Unpredictable; dangerous for travel and resource gathering
- **Plot relevance:** A spatial magic research accident in a dungeon is what causes the Arc 2 disaster — the researcher underestimated the complexity of dungeon spatial mechanics, created a cascading collapse, and randomly displaced people including Bon's family

All dungeon ruins, spatial folding infrastructure, and anchor structures are **leftover technology from the pre-disaster civilization.** The current world doesn't fully understand what it's built on top of.

## 8. Vaela's Anchor Network

**What the anchors are:** Ancient monuments, standing stones, and memorial statues distributed across the world — leftover infrastructure from the pre-disaster civilization.

**How they work:**
- When people interact with or near an anchor, Vaela instantly observes their entire life memories
- She has been doing this for centuries
- She can create subtle emotional resonance through anchored objects, influencing events without direct intervention

**The problem:**
- After centuries of absorbing others' memories, she can no longer clearly distinguish between observed memories and her own experiences
- She does not know if the people she saved in the original catastrophe betrayed her deliberately or not — that memory is buried under everyone else's

**Vaela and Bon specifically:**
- Near an anchor, she encounters Bon's memories of Japan — a world outside her network
- This is the first evidence she has that other worlds exist and that consciousness can transfer between them
- She cannot fully read his memories (interference from the inter-world transfer)
- This gives her reason to keep him alive rather than eliminate him: she must keep returning to study him
- His unabsorbed memories remind her what it felt like to have a memory that was only hers

## 9. Elven Medicine (Brynth)

Two distinct healing disciplines unavailable elsewhere:
1. **Ancient herbal-alchemical medicine** — botanical knowledge lost to other races
2. **Restorative Magic (biological school)** — unique to elves; no equivalent exists elsewhere

Both have hard limits: cannot reverse death, cannot cure soul-rooted illness.

Their dual mastery is why Brynth is the only destination for Elena's cure in Arc 1.
