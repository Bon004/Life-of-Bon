## Arc 2 — The Separation Crisis
**Age:** 11–12 | **Chapters:** 26–35
**Goal:** Survive the disaster; face the consequences of his failure

### The Disaster
- Dungeon spatial magic researcher attempts to create controlled portals
- Underestimates complexity; creates cascading spatial collapse
- Randomly displaces people through unstable dimensional pockets

### What Actually Happens (Canon)
- Bon's fire magic misfires during the collapse, putting people in danger
- Leon runs toward the danger Bon created — his instinct to protect is what kills him
- The spatial collapse physically separates Bon before he can reach Leon
- Stranded, guilty, paralyzed — a mercenary band finds him
- **The Turn:** He doesn't choose them over his family, but he doesn't fight to get back immediately either. That failure mirrors Japan.

### Survival Goals
- Survive first week in hostile region
- Find shelter within 3 days
- Earn 100 silver for supplies
- Locate information about family

**Bon's state at end:** Leon is dead. Elena is enslaved somewhere unknown. Bon is alone, consumed by guilt, and has already begun the same disappearing act he used in Japan. The pattern is alive.

## Arc 6 — Final Confrontation
**Age:** 23+ | **Chapters:** 131–150+
**Goal:** Stop Vaela's reality restructuring

### Climax Structure
- **Physical battle:** Champion's (or equivalent) enhanced power vs. Bon's accumulated growth
- **Emotional battle:** Reaching through denial and desperation to the person underneath
- **Resolution:** Letting go — breaking Vaela's power
- **Aftermath:** Bittersweet victory — reality saved, but someone is lost

### Vaela's Arc
Her plan (restructure reality so consciousness persists without physical form) is real — she genuinely believes it. But it grew on top of something simpler: she was betrayed by the people she destroyed herself to save, and she can no longer remember if the original reason still applies. The confrontation forces both of them to face what they've been running from.

**Bon's state at end:** Acceptance. Whatever form his ending takes — the emotional resolution is the same.
