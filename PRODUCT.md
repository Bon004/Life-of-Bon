# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary user: a single writer (currently Esteban) working alone, often at night, in focused creative sessions. Building and organizing a story across characters, world details, plot arcs, quotes, and ideas.

Future users: other writers across any genre — fantasy, fiction, memoir, biography, research papers, scientific writing. The app must feel equally at home for someone drafting a novel and someone organizing academic notes.

Context: the user is deep in a creative or intellectual task. They open the app to think, not to be impressed by it.

Job to be done: organize raw notes into structured categories, use AI to surface connections, develop narrative or structural clarity across a large body of content.

## Product Purpose

StoryForge is an AI-assisted writing workspace. It processes raw notes (pasted text or uploaded files) through Claude AI and organizes them into a visual kanban canvas of categorized cards (Characters, World Building, Plot, Quotes, Ideas). It also provides a writing editor, character profiles, arc timelines, and an AI chat assistant.

Success looks like: a writer sits down, opens the app, and immediately knows where everything is. The tool stays out of the way until needed.

## Brand Personality

Focused, sophisticated, calm. The tool should feel intentional and premium — like a serious creative workspace that a professional would trust with their work.

Voice: quiet confidence. It does not announce itself. It just works, and works well.

Emotional goal: the user should feel grounded and clear-headed when using it, not stimulated or distracted.

## Anti-references

- **Startup SaaS aesthetics (2023-2024):** purple gradients, neon glows, glassmorphism cards, aurora backgrounds, hero-metric grids, gradient text. The current D1 polish block is this exact pattern.
- **Corporate enterprise software:** gray toolbars, dense UI, dated visual language, no personality.
- **Toy or student project:** rough spacing, inconsistent type, no visual hierarchy, looks unfinished.
- **Social media / content creator apps:** bright, casual, engagement-bait layout.

## Design Principles

1. **The story is the product.** The UI exists to serve the writing, never to compete with it. Every decorative choice must earn its place by serving clarity or mood.
2. **Cohesion over cleverness.** Every visual decision should feel like part of one considered system. No trend-assembled UI.
3. **Genre-neutral seriousness.** The design must feel equally appropriate for a fantasy epic and a research paper. No aesthetic that locks it to one domain.
4. **Built to last.** Avoid anything that will read as dated in two years. Trend-resistant over trend-forward.
5. **Earn the dark.** The dark theme is chosen because this is focused, late-night creative work — not because dark interfaces look cool. Every surface decision should reinforce that intent.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Basic level for now: sufficient text contrast, no information conveyed by color alone, legible type sizes. Revisit when the app expands to a wider audience.
